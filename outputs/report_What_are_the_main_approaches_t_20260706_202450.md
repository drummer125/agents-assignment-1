# Literature Review on Approaches to Building AI Agents That Can Reason and Act

## Executive Summary
This literature review explores the foundational approaches to building AI agents capable of reasoning and acting. The synthesis of 15 curated papers reveals that effective reasoning models, learning paradigms, and hybrid methodologies are central to enhancing the decision-making capabilities of AI agents. Key challenges related to logistical complexity and ethical considerations also emerge as significant barriers in the development process.

## Introduction
The question at hand is: "What are the main approaches to building AI agents that can reason and act?" AI agents are defined as intelligent systems designed to perform tasks autonomously, while reasoning involves the processes by which agents make logical inferences and decisions. Action refers to the execution of these decisions in physical or digital environments. The literature offers perspectives on various reasoning models, learning paradigms, and hybrid methodologies while highlighting the inherent challenges faced in developing such agents.

## Methodology
The review's findings are drawn from a well-curated corpus of 15 papers focusing on the interplay of reasoning and action in AI agents. The search strategy encompassed sub-questions regarding models of AI reasoning, the contribution of learning paradigms, the role of logical reasoning, the effectiveness of hybrid approaches, and the challenges faced in AI development. 

## Findings
### Models of AI Reasoning
The foundational architectures and methodologies for building AI agents play critical roles in their reasoning capabilities. Agents can decompose tasks based on specific architectures, thus enabling effective reasoning about their actions. Understanding desires, intentions, and beliefs is crucial for the development of computational agents capable of reasoning effectively [wooldridge_1995][xi_2023_survey].

### Learning Paradigms and Their Impact
Different learning paradigms, such as reinforcement and supervised learning, significantly influence the decision-making capabilities of AI agents. Reinforcement learning allows agents to optimize their actions based on feedback from their environment, which is crucial for adaptive behaviors [xi_2023_survey][wooldridge_1995].

### The Role of Logical Reasoning
Logical reasoning is fundamental in the decision-making processes of AI agents, particularly in language models. It facilitates memory management and task tracking, enabling agents to act effectively across diverse situations. Chain-of-thought prompting exemplifies this by allowing models to systematically reason through problems [react_2023][cot_2022].

### Hybrid Approaches to Reasoning
Hybrid methodologies that combine various strategies, such as heuristic-guided search and multi-agent systems, enhance the reasoning capabilities of AI agents. The Tree of Thoughts approach embodies such a hybrid methodology, incorporating multiple steps in problem-solving processes [tot_2023][camel_2023].

### Challenges and Limitations
Developing reasoning-capable AI agents encounters significant challenges, including managing the complexity of logical structures. Ethical considerations and scalability issues frequently inhibit the practical deployment of reasoning-based AI. These challenges necessitate innovative approaches to design and implementation [xi_2023_survey][wooldridge_1995].

## Discussion
The literature consistently emphasizes the importance of logical reasoning and learning paradigms in empowering AI agents' decision-making processes. While there is a consensus among researchers on these foundational aspects, the existing corpus does not extensively cover the implications of ethical considerations on AI reasoning. Furthermore, despite discussing hybrid approaches, a gap exists in empirical studies exploring their effectiveness in real-world applications.

## Conclusion
This review highlights essential approaches to building reasoning-capable AI agents, underscoring the relevance of hybrid methodologies, reasoning models, and learning paradigms. However, significant challenges remain, particularly concerning the ethical dimensions and practical execution of these methodologies, suggesting areas for further research and exploration.
